# Critical Business Flows: Automation Exercise Site

Based on a detailed exploration of [Automation Exercise](https://automationexercise.com/), I have identified the most critical business flows that should be automated to ensure site stability and a seamless user experience.

---

## 1. Primary Conversion: Guest-to-Customer Journey
This is the most critical flow as it simulates the path of a new user making a purchase.

**Steps:**
1. Navigate to Home Page.
2. Search for a specific product (e.g., "Blue Top").
3. Add the product to the cart.
4. Click "Proceed to Checkout."
5. On the "Register / Login" prompt, choose **Signup**.
6. Fill in Account Information, Address Details, and Contact Info.
7. Click "Create Account" and "Continue."
8. Return to Cart and Proceed to Checkout.
9. Verify Address Details and Order Summary.
10. Enter Payment Details and Confirm Order.
11. **Verify "Order Placed!" success message.**

**Why Critical:** Validates the entire sales funnel and the transition from guest to registered user.

---

## 2. Inventory & Search Reliability
Ensures that users can find what they are looking for across different categories.

**Steps:**
1. Navigate to "Products" page.
2. Use the Search Bar for a broad term (e.g., "Dress").
3. Verify all returned items contain the keyword.
4. Use the Sidebar to filter by **Women > Tops**.
5. Verify that only "Tops" are displayed.
6. Click "View Product" for the first item and verify price/availability details.

**Why Critical:** Search and Filtering are the primary ways users discover products. Failure here leads to lost sales.

---

## 3. Shopping Cart Logic & Edge Cases
Validates the state management of the cart.

**Steps:**
1. Add Item A (Quantity 1).
2. Add Item B (Quantity 2).
3. Navigate to Cart.
4. Increase Quantity of Item A to 3.
5. Remove Item B.
6. **Verify Total Price updates correctly.**
7. Close browser and reopen (Persistent Cart check if supported).

**Why Critical:** Cart calculations and item persistence are common points of failure in e-commerce apps.

## 4. Customer Support Channel (Contact Us)
Ensures users can reach out for help.

**Steps:**
1. Click on "Contact Us."
2. Fill in Name, Email, Subject, and Message.
3. Upload a sample document (File Upload field).
4. Click "Submit."
5. Handle the browser alert (OK).
6. **Verify "Success! Your details have been submitted successfully" message.**

**Why Critical:** If the contact form fails, the business loses the ability to resolve customer issues, leading to poor reviews.

---

## 5. Security & Account Cleanup
Protects the platform from invalid access and manages test data.

**Steps:**
1. **Positive Login**: Login with valid credentials and verify "Logged in as [User]."
2. **Negative Login**: Attempt login with wrong password and verify error message.
3. **Logout**: Ensure session is cleared.
4. **Delete Account**: Navigate to "Delete Account" and verify the account is successfully removed from the system.

**Why Critical:** Essential for security testing and for ensuring tests leave the environment in a clean state (Teardown).
